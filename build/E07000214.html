Fastest-growing places

09 February 2021


The population of England and Wales has increased by 3.7 million in the 10 years leading up to the 2011 Census. Using the first results from this census, we look at which places have seen the biggest changes, which populations are ageing fastest, and how your area compares to other areas.


Surrey Heath

Scroll to read about Surrey Heath

In Surrey Heath, the population has increased from 80,400 in 2001 to 86,200 in 2011, a rise of 7.2%. This is slightly lower than the average for England (7.9%).

While the population has increased, the amount of land in England has not. Some areas have seen their population density increase much more than others.

As of 2011, Surrey Heath is the 28th most densely populated of the South East's 64 districts, with around 5 people living on each football-pitch-sized area of land.

In Islington, the population works out at around 74 per pitch.

At the other end of the population density scale, the amount of land in the rural district of Eden works out at nearly 9 pitches per resident.

Around 51% of the population of England is female and 49% is male. However, women tend to live longer than men, so areas with an older population typically have more females than males.

In the 65 years and over age group, Surrey Heath has a split of 14,500 females to 9,600 males.

Every local authority district in Wales has more females than males, with the most balanced place being Wrexham. The places with the highest ratio of females to males in England is Conwy, which has 106 females for every 100 males.
